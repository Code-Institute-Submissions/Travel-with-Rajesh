Everest Region

Gallery
Everst Base Camp
  • Duration: 14 days
  • Max Elevation: 5364 m
  • Difficulty: Medium to Hard
  • Accessible: For everyone how likes to challange them selfs and how are in good shape.
  • During: This 14 day trek starts with a good night rest in kathmandu. The next day you will have an electrifying flight to the airport in Lukla, which hangs on the edge of a cliff at 2860m. From Lukla, the gateway to the Everest region, the trip winds through the floral and faunal richness of Sagarmatha National Park, while at the same time following the spectacular views of Himalayan giants including Mt. Everest (8848m), Mt. Lhotse (8586m), Mt. Cho Oyu (8188m) and Mt. Ama Dablam (6812m). Not just the natural grandeur, once the trail starts ascending, you will also get deeper and deeper into the astounding cultural highlights of highland Sherpa villages. The monasteries, stony houses, Buddhist prayers flags and engraved mani walls throughout the trip will give you the feel of a mystical journey. With every step ahead, you will get closer to Everest Base Camp which lies at the foot of the Khumbu Icefall. From there continues your glacial walk to Kalapattar (5545m), the most scenic viewpoint to savor the eternal beauty of Everest and other Khumbu peaks. Finally, after relishing the sublime beauty of Khumbu your journey culminates and the trip retraces back to Lukla.

Annapurna Base Camp
  • Duration: 10-12 days
  • Max Elevation: 4130 m
  • Difficulty: Medium
  • Accessible: For everyone who enjoys an adventurous walk through a scenery of the mountain.
  • During: This 10 to 12 days trekking will take you through rice fields, waterfalls and rivers, the rhododendron Forest and typical Nepali villages with the beautiful view of Annapurna Himalaya Range. The path will lead you to a beautiful village called Ghorepani (2810m). This village belongs to one of the ethinic group call Magar and from here you will have an impressive view of the rhododendron forest and the Himalaya. It is worth the climb to Poon Hill in the early morning for a panoramic view of the sunrise and 14 different mountains. Another village you will travel to is Chhomrong and it is a beautiful Gurung village which lies in the lap of Annapurna South, with views of many moutains including Mt. Himal Chuli and Mt. Fish Tail. The final destination of this Trek is Annapurna Base Camp and it is surrounded by the Annapurna Himalayan Range with the altitude of 4130m. Jhinu Danda is a good place to let go of all your tiredness of walking with a hot spring. The trail goes back to Nayapul along the river call madi which comes all the way from Annapurna South.

Mardi Himal
  • Duration: 5 days
  • Max Elevation: 3700 m
  • Difficulty: Easy to Medium
  • Accessible: For everyone who enjoys an adventurous walk through the scenery of mountains.

Phoon Hill
  • Duration: 5 days
  • Max Elevation: 3210 m
  • Difficulty: Easy
  • Accessible: For everyone who enjoys an adventurous walk through the scenery of mountains.
  • During: This 5 days trek will take you to rice fields, a waterfall and rivers, the rhododendron forest and a typical Nepali village with a beautiful view of the Annapurna Himalaya Range. the trail leads you to a beautiful village called Ghorepani (2810 m). This village belongs to one of the ethnic groups called Magar. From here you will have an impressive view of the rhododendron forest and the Himalaya. Langtang Region

Gallery
Langtang Valley
  • Duration: 7-9 days
  • Max Elevation: 3850 m
  • Difficulty: Easy to Medium
  • Accessible: For everyone who enjoys an adventurous walk through the scenery of mountains.
  • During: This 7 to 9 days trekking in the Langtang valley offers a small view of the Tibetan culture and rituals, as well as a view of different landscapes. You get to see the rhododendrons forest Valleys, rivers, waterfalls and Glacier at a low comfortable elevation. Furthermore, there are many mountains that you get to see along the trail. For example Mt. Langtang Lirung (7246m.), Mt. Gang Chhenpo (6388m), Mt. Naya Gang (5846m) and Mt. Dorje Lakpa( 6966m). The trail goes through the rhododendron forest alongside the Langtang river that flows to Mt. Kyanjing gompa (3850m). You will visit a Buddhist monastery and the oldest yak cheese factory of the world. It is worth spending one extra day in Kyanjing gompa to hike some of the following trails the Kyanjing RI, the Cherko RI or the Lang SE SA RI kharka. The trail will go back the same way to Shyapru Bensi or Dhunche.

Manaslu Round
  • Duration: 14 to 16 days
  • Max Elevation: 5106 m
  • Difficulty: Medium to Hard
  • Accessible:
  • During: This 15 to 16 day trek will guide you through some beautiful Gurung villages and the Tibetan culture,it will bring you to the peaceful Budhi Gandaki valley, which is located in the Gorkha region. This is the land from where the first king of Nepal started to creating Nepal. You can enjoy the relaxing sounds of the river while you pass local people working on the rice fields. futher you will see green hills, waterfalls and a hot spring. While walking through the forest you will see wildlife like birds, deer, monkeys and the Himalayan blue sheep. During this hike you will get to know the typical way of Nepali life with different ethnic groups. The trail goes through SamaGaun, where you take one extra day to walk up to ManasluBasecamp (4500 m). Mount Manaslu is also known as the mountain of Spirit. From this place you can visit the Glacier Lake. You continue the path through Samdo to the highest point of our hike, Larke La (5106 m). To get down to BesiSahar, you will walk on the Annapurna Circuit trail for a few days.
  • minimum with 2 persons because it's a restricted area (special permit needed)
